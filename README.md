## ğŸ“Š Key Findings & Data Insights
The analysis of the datasets revealed several critical distinctions between the two cities:

1. **Extreme Seasonality in Milan:** The **Time Series Analysis** clearly shows that Milan experiences significant spikes in PM2.5 levels during the **winter months (November to February)**. These levels are often double those of Paris during the same period, likely due to the geographical "Po Valley" effect which traps pollutants.

2. **Statistical Volatility:** The **Box Plot** analysis indicates that Milan not only has a higher median pollution level but also a much wider interquartile range (IQR) and more frequent extreme outliers. This suggests that "unhealthy" air days are more common and severe in Milan than in Paris.

3. **Distribution Patterns:** The **Frequency Histogram** shows a distinct right-skewed distribution for Milan. While Paris maintains a more stable and "lean" distribution toward lower PM2.5 values, Milan has a significant "long tail," representing frequent exposure to high-intensity pollution events.
